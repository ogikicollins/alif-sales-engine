# Cold Call Scripts
> Collins Ogiki | ALIF Agency
> Three scripts: warm call, cold call, no-show recovery

---

## Before Any Call — 60-Second Prep

Pull up HubSpot. Find their record. Know:
- Their first name + company name
- What email they replied to and exactly what they said
- One specific thing about their business (their Instagram, a recent ad, their website)
- Which ALIF case study matches their vertical

If you can't find one specific thing to open with, don't call yet. Do the 60-second research first.

---

## Script 1: Warm Call — Positive Replier Who Hasn't Booked

**When:** They replied positively to an email. More than 24 hours have passed. They haven't booked.

**Opening:**
"Hey [First Name], it's Collins — I'm on the sales team at ALIF Agency. You replied to one of our emails a couple of days ago. You have a second?"

*[If no: "No problem — what time today works better? I'll call you then." Lock in a time. Hang up.]*

*[If yes:]*

"I didn't want to just fire another email at you. You mentioned [quote what they said exactly]. Tell me a bit more about that — what's the situation right now?"

*[Shut up. Let them talk. Don't interrupt. The longer they talk, the higher your close rate.]*

**After they explain — qualify in two questions:**

"Got it. Who's currently handling that for you — is it in-house or are you working with anyone outside?"

*[Listen.]*

"And what's the main thing you're trying to fix or improve in the next 90 days? Like if we were talking again in 3 months and everything had gone right — what would have changed?"

*[This is the golden question. Their answer becomes your close.]*

**Bridge to booking:**

"Okay — so what we typically do is a focused 20-minute call where I walk you through what we've seen work for [similar company/vertical]. Not a pitch — just want to make sure what we do is actually relevant to your situation before I take any more of your time. Are you free [give specific day] at [specific time] or [alternative]?"

*[Two options only. Never ask "what works for you."]*

*[On yes: "Perfect. I'll text you the Calendly link right now so it's in your calendar."]*

## Script 2: Cold Call — Net New Prospect

**When:** They've received emails but never replied, OR you're calling before emailing.

**Opening:**
"Hey [First Name], it's Collins from ALIF Agency in Dubai. I'm going to be straight with you — this is a cold call. You've got 30 seconds to tell me it's not relevant. Fair?"

*[Most people laugh. They stay on. The transparency disarms them.]*

"The reason I'm calling specifically — I looked at [their Instagram / their ads / their website] and I noticed [one specific observation, e.g., 'your last four ads have been running for 60+ days without a creative change, which is usually where performance drops']. I wondered if that's something you're aware of or if it's on the list of things to fix."

*[Ask, then stop.]*

*[If yes, it's a problem:]*
"Okay — we work with [similar brand] on exactly this. They went from [result]. I think there's a similar opportunity here. Is it worth 20 minutes this week to see if it applies to you?"

*[If no:]*
"What's the thing that's actually keeping you up at night with [marketing/ads/social] right now?"

*[Pivot to their real pain. Qualify from there.]*

## Script 3: No-Show Recovery

**When:** They booked. They didn't show. Call within 10 minutes.

"Hey [First Name], it's Collins from ALIF — we had a call just now and I think something came up on your end. No problem at all. Are you still free to jump on, or should we find a new time this week?"

*[Two outcomes: they jump on now, or you rebook. Both are wins. Never express frustration.]*

## Objection Handlers

**"We're already working with an agency"**
"Good — means you're already investing in this. I'm not asking you to switch anything. One question: are you happy with what you're getting from them? [Their answer tells you everything.] If you're not, it's worth 20 minutes to see what different looks like."

**"Send me an email"**
"I will. So I can make it worth reading — what's the one thing you're most focused on solving in the next quarter?" [Get the answer. Then say:] "Perfect. I'll send something specific to that. What's the best email?"

**"How did you get my number?"**
"LinkedIn/Apollo/public research — we're pretty targeted about who we reach out to. You match a very specific profile. Is that a problem?" [Most say no. A few say yes. Cut them loose — they're not your buyer.]

**"Not interested"**
"Completely fair. Can I ask what's making it a no right now? Not trying to change your mind — genuinely want to understand so I don't waste your time in the future." [Listen. This is competitive intelligence.]

**"We don't have budget"**
"Understood. When does that conversation happen internally? If the answer is in 3 months, I'd rather call you then than have a conversation that goes nowhere now." [Book a follow-up call for 90 days out. Log it in HubSpot.]
