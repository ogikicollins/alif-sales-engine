# Voicemail SOPs
> Collins Ogiki | ALIF Agency
> Three voicemail scripts — when to use each, word for word

---

## The Rule

Leave a voicemail only on call attempt 2 or 3. Not attempt 1 — too soon, no credibility built. Not attempt 4+ — you're chasing, and they can tell.

Every voicemail should create an open loop that can only be closed by calling you back. Not by doing nothing. Not by deleting the message.

Keep it under 25 seconds. Time yourself.

---

## Voicemail 1: The Competitor Trick

**When to use:** Cold prospects who haven't replied to emails. Best used Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11AM prospect local time.

**The script:**

"Hey [First Name], it's Collins at ALIF — I was calling regarding [Competitor Name]. Give me a ring back when you get a chance: [number]. That's [repeat number slowly]. Thanks."

**That's it. Nothing else.**

**What happens next:** They call back curious. When they do:

"Hey [First Name], thanks for calling back. So I was looking at [Competitor Name]'s setup alongside yours — specifically [one specific thing, e.g. 'their AI search presence' or 'their ad creative strategy'] — and I noticed something I think is relevant to [Company Name]. Is now a good time?"

**Why it works:** "Regarding your competitor" creates an information gap that the human brain cannot leave unresolved. They will call back to find out what you know. The callback rate on this voicemail is 3–5x higher than any other voicemail approach.

**Do not:** Explain anything on the voicemail. The information lives in the callback. The moment you explain it on the voicemail, the loop closes and they don't call.

---

## Voicemail 2: The Audit Hook

**When to use:** Warm prospects — they replied to an email but haven't booked. After 2 unanswered call attempts.

**Prep before leaving this:** Actually look at something on their business. Their Instagram, their active ads, their website. Find one specific thing. This is not optional.

**The script:**

"Hey [First Name], Collins from ALIF — I took a quick look at [Company Name]'s [Instagram/ad account/website] and noticed [one specific, concrete finding, e.g. 'your last three ad creatives have been running 50+ days — that's usually the point where CPL starts climbing']. Thought it was worth a conversation. Give me a call back: [number]. That's [number]. Speak soon."

**Why it works:** You've done work for them before they've given you anything. It signals that a conversation with you will be valuable, not transactional. The specific finding proves you're not running a spray-and-pray operation.

**Do not** leave a generic version of this. If you can't find a specific observation, use Voicemail 1 instead.

---

## Voicemail 3: The Peer-to-Peer Direct

**When to use:** Senior decision makers — CMO, Founder, CEO level. When you want to signal peer-level confidence, not sales rep energy.

**The script:**

"[First Name], Collins from ALIF in Dubai. Quick call — think there's something worth looking at for [Company Name] based on what we've been seeing in [their industry/market] lately. Happy to be brief. [Number]. Thanks."

**Tone:** Calm. Confident. Slightly understated. Not enthusiastic. Not formal. Peer-to-peer.

**Under 20 seconds.** Time yourself. Every extra second reduces callback rate.

**Why it works:** Confidence is contagious. The tone signals that you don't need the meeting — you think it's worth their time. That framing reverses the power dynamic in a cold call context.

## Voicemail Sequencing

| Attempt | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Call 1 | No voicemail. Just call. |
| Call 2 | Leave Voicemail 1 or 2 (based on warm/cold) |
| Call 3 | Leave Voicemail 3 |
| Call 4 | No voicemail. Last attempt. If no answer, send final email. |

After 4 attempts with no response: move to 60-day nurture in HubSpot. Tag "Called x4 — No Answer."

---

## After a Callback — The First 5 Seconds

The moment they call back, they're in a different mental state than a cold call. They called you. The power dynamic is different. Use it.

"Hey [First Name] — thanks for calling back. [One breath.] So..."

Then go straight into the substance. No "how are you" — they called back because of what you said. Respect their time.
